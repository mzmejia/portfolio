Client Project - 2020

Spark FM Case Study

the summary

As New England’s #1 radio station for Urban and Caribbean music, Spark FM continues to connect and give back to their community.

My team was tasked with a full redesign of their native mobile application to provide a platform that achieves their goal of being more than just a music application.

the problem

Due to difficulties faced when using the current application, users could no longer support the station.

Our users needed a convenient way to listen and engage with Spark FM so that they could connect with, and support, others in their local community while accessing the music they love and the content they craved.

- Unable to access information quickly

- Could not engage or interact with on-air personalities

- Unable to listen to the live-stream of music and shows

discovery

We empathized with Spark FM users and conducted remote interviews to understand their needs, desires, and pain points.

- 13 interviews with listeners and artists – 4 conducted myself – to help shape our solution

- The average length of interviews lasted 40-45 minutes to best understand our users’ needs

- After reviewing each others interviews, we gathered 905 initial insights

user quotes

"Human contact on the radio makes the days a little bit easier to get through."

"…I want apps to have more content to keep you engaged."

"You can find music anywhere but radio gives access to local content and news."

"I like that Spark FM is community-based and local artist-focused."

Define

Altering the process of synthesization, to handle our initial 905 insights, our primary persona was a result of 54 statements

Filtered down our initial 905 insights to 316 based on a value scale to ensure we worked with relevant information. From there, we utilized an affinity map to sort our insights through 3 different rounds of categorization.

Main user needs

Quick access
It was important for our users to get access to the content they wanted quickly

Stay up to date
Unable to find the unique content and music desired elsewhere, users wanted the latest music from Sparks local artists

Engagement
Users wanted the ability to walk through the application and access content while listening to the live stream radio

Support the community
Appreciative of Spark FM’s focus of bringing the community together, users desired to be informed and support others in their local community

COMPETITIVE AND COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS

With the scope of the project in mind, we narrowed down our solution to design a product that met our users’ needs and satisfied our client’s goals

- Used a MVP model due to financial and time constraints (3 weeks)

- Consulted with an expert developer to create a realistic first iteration of our solution

- Used data from our competitive analysis as a blueprint for features to include

solution/design

Our solution – a platform for community engagement through music and radio.

By designing a mobile application for Spark FM’s music content and community engagement, we believe their users will have a convenient way to access the music they love, stay informed about local news, and virtually connect with others in their area.

Homepage / Live Radio

1. Top navigation
With our users’ preferring to search for the content they desired, we decided to include a search button in a recognizable and accessible location

2. Sub navigation
With a desire to use an app that was quick and convenient, we decided to design a “tabbed” sub-navigation that housed sub-pages within their parent pages

3. Listen now
Spark FM’s prime attraction is their local radio station, and so we provided users with the ability to instantly launch the radio

4. Mini schedule
Our users needed to stay up to date on Spark FM shows, and so we decided to list information on the current and future slot of radio shows on the home page

5. Bottom navigation
We designed our main navigation to be accessible, placing it in a convenient and familiar location. We included the ability to easily launch the chatroom, as our users enjoyed interacting with the radio station

Live Radio Player

1. Content image
To visually display the current show, we followed a common practice of placing a large picture of the content being played to keep our users informed and engaged

2. Content description
We showcased the information of the current show being played because our users desired to support Spark FM and its many artists and DJs. We decided to use a simple, hierarchical layout to display important information

3. Playback controls
With our users’ familiarity with music apps of all kinds, we decided to maintain consistency with our playback controls. Our design focused on simplifying the interface to help our users from being overwhelmed with choices

4. Mini schedule
Our users needed to stay up to date on Spark FM shows, and so we decided to list information on the current and future slot of radio shows on the home page

Playlists

1. Page title
With the amount of information and pages within our app, we realized it was necessary to include another form of confirmation for our users. We replaced the Spark FM logo with the title of every parent page (apart from the homepage)

2. New music
Our users needed to stay up to date with artists content, so we designed our first playlist category to encompass Spark FM’s newest curated playlists

3. Featured creators
Desiring to find and support local talent, our users rely on music platforms to introduce them to new music and artists, which was the foundation for creating a category showcasing featured playlists created by Spark FM artists

4. Content display
To maintain consistency with our designs, as well as the “norm” of the music industry, we designed our displayed playlists in a simple and confusion-free way

5. Mini player
Because our users desired to engage with content throughout the app while listening to music, we designed a simplistic mini player. Only including necessary functionality to limit confusion, we wanted to allow users the opportunity to remain informed and in control of their music as they interacted within the app

Individual Playlist (Soca Hits)

1. Playlist description
We designed our individual playlist pages to have an initial description section to provide context

2. Play button
To alleviate confusion, and to highlight our users’ desire for convenience, we included a large play button that stands apart from the background

3. Song details
Staying consistent with our previous designs, we decided to create rows for each song before providing each row with padding to ensure there is enough clickable space

4. Inline advertisements
Recognizing the needs of the business, we decided to unobtrusively place inline advertisements to keep our users’ experience streamlined

Chat Room

1. Host information
We designed a header section that informs our users on which artist/DJ is currently online to inform them about who they are engaging with. We also provided a small indicator of active users to satisfy their desires of connecting with the community

2. Received messages
When designing the messages screen, we decided to showcase the most important elements needed when communicating with each other virtually: name, timestamp, profile pictures, and the message itself

3. Sent messages
In order to provide visual confirmation about when a users’ message has successfully been sent, we used a soft yellow background to maintain consistency with the color scheme, while providing contrast from surrounding messages
